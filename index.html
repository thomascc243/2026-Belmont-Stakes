The Formula

How value scores are built

The formula finds horses the public is systematically under-betting in exotic pools relative to their actual ability. In a 20-horse field, the public piles money onto a handful of names — compressing their exacta and trifecta payouts. That creates scarcity opportunity for overlooked horses with legitimate form.

Value Score = (Implied Probability × 0.35) + (Pool Scarcity × 0.40) + (Payout Multiplier × 0.25) + (Post Position Bonus × 0.05)
Implied Probability
Win probability from morning-line odds: 1 ÷ (odds+1) × 100
Pool Scarcity
How under-bet in exotic pools: 100 − estimated pool inclusion %
Payout Multiplier
Odds-based return potential — rewards 10-1 through 30-1 range
Post Position Bonus
+5 for posts 5–15 (historically optimal) · −5 for posts 1–3 and 17+

Post position is factored into the 2026 formula specifically because the draw matters enormously in a 20-horse field. No horse has won from post 1 since 1986. Posts 5 and 8 have produced the most Derby winners historically. Speed figures also serve as a binary filter — every Kentucky Derby winner since 2000 has posted at least one 100+ Beyer.

Honest Limitations

What the formula doesn't know

Renegade at post 1 is genuinely talented. Irad Ortiz Jr. is the best jockey in the country and Todd Pletcher has won the Derby twice. The rail curse is real but not absolute. The formula skips him based on pool saturation + post history, not because he can't win. If he wins, you cash nothing — that's the calculated risk.

Further Ado's 106 Beyer is the best figure in the field — five points better than anyone else. That kind of performance wins Derbies. Post 18 is the genuine concern, not his form. He's in Trifecta C specifically because the formula can't discount him completely.

So Happy's stamina is a real question. His sire Runhappy was a champion sprinter and the 1¼-mile Derby distance is a stretch. He's in the underneath slots, not on top, precisely because of this uncertainty.

Pool % are estimates. Real tote data updates live on your app from May 1 at 9 AM. Check Will Pays or Probables on race day — if The Puma is under 12% of trifecta pool inclusion that's your strongest scarcity confirmation signal.

The formula's edge is process, not prediction. It identifies horses where the public is systematically mispricing probability. When they fire, the scarcity means the payout reflects genuine market inefficiency. Good luck on May 2. 🌹
