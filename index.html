The Plan for Today
Last time out at Saratoga on July 17th, the formula cashed three races — Discotheque in Race 2, Lexi's Charm in Race 4, and Antietam in Race 7. We spent $235 and got back $136. Three winners, still a loss. The problem wasn't the picks. It was the structure. We spread money too thin across too many races, and the payouts on the races we hit — particularly that $4.62 exacta in Race 7 with the 2-1 favorite sitting second — couldn't overcome the bleed.

Today the formula runs differently. We are hunting one good payout, not trying to cash nine races. The budget is $186 total, but it is not split evenly. Races 1, 2, 4, and 6 get action-only bets — small, low-cost tickets just to stay engaged. Race 3 gets a medium play on the Irad/Sharp angle. The real money goes into three races where the payout structure is right:

Race 5 — the 2-year-old maiden with first-time starters is the chaos race. Superfectas in these fields regularly pay $200 to $2,000. We're putting $48 here almost entirely into the superfecta.

Race 7 — the Suzie O'Cain Stakes $200K is the feature. Eight NY-bred fillies, Celtic Dawn (Irad) vs. All of It (Franco), Prat at 5-1 underneath. If the two favorites run 1-2 the trifecta pays nothing. But if Considerate City or New York Special sneaks in, this pays big. We skip putting All of It on top and attack with spread coverage.

Race 8 — the $130K AOC 7-furlong fillies race has the best odds distribution on the card: 7-2, 5-2, 7-2, and 6-1 all live. Any combination of three that avoids the obvious chalks pays meaningfully. We upgrade to $1 trifecta combos here.
